# Control Interface Options

When you need **software control** of pan/tilt or other actuators and already
have RC gear (e.g. FlySky transmitter and receiver), you have several practical
options. Do **not** attempt RF protocol emulation (e.g. FlySky AFHDS); it is
hard, brittle, and unnecessary.

## When you have no microcontroller

If you do not yet have an ESP32, Arduino, or similar:

- **USB servo driver board** — A PCA9685-based (or similar) **USB servo
  controller** plugs into the laptop and drives servos over USB via serial or
  I2C. Laptop → USB → servo driver → servos. No MCU or custom firmware; your app
  sends commands to the driver. When you later add an ESP32, switch to
  SerialTransport or Wi-Fi and keep the same supervisor/contract.
- **Old flashable flight controller** — An old drone flight controller with
  STM32, USB, and motor/servo outputs can act as a **USB→PWM bridge**. Flash
  Betaflight/INAV or use MAVLink; PC → USB → flight controller → PWM → servos.
  No RF and no separate MCU purchase. See
  [Salvage and zero-budget hardware](../hardware/salvage-and-zero-budget-hardware.mdx)
  for which boards are promising.

In all cases, build the **software pipeline first** (detection → tracking →
supervisor → transport); use SimulatedTransport until hardware is available. See
[Virtual turret POC](../../engineering/playbooks/virtual-turret-poc.mdx).

## Option 1: Replace receiver with MCU

Use an ESP32, Arduino, or similar microcontroller to **output PWM directly to
servos**. The receiver is removed; the MCU receives commands over USB, serial,
or Wi-Fi and drives servos (or a PCA9685). This is the recommended path for
Phase-1 turrets. See [Actuator](../../engineering/common/actuator.mdx) and
[NetSnare Lite (turret)](../../engineering/phase1/products/netsnare-lite-turret.mdx).

## Option 2: Hardware switch (receiver vs MCU)

Keep both the receiver and an MCU. A **physical switch** selects which source
drives the servos: either the receiver (manual RF) or the MCU (software
commands). Wiring and switching logic must ensure only one source is connected
at a time and that power and ground are shared correctly.

## Option 3: Analog injection (spoof transmitter sticks)

Keep the transmitter and receiver unchanged. **Spoof the transmitter’s stick
inputs** with a voltage from an MCU (DAC or PWM filtered to analog, or a digital
potentiometer). The transmitter still sends RF; the receiver still outputs PWM
to the servos. You do not touch the RF layer.

**Minimum hardware:** A small MCU (e.g. ESP8266, ESP32, Arduino Nano, STM32),
two analog outputs (DAC or filtered PWM), one physical mode switch, and common
ground with the transmitter.

**Safety (mandatory):**

- **Physical toggle:** MANUAL = real potentiometers (operator controls sticks).
  AUTO = MCU injects voltages (software controls). Never remove the ability to
  revert to manual.
- **Failsafe:** If the MCU stops updating or fails, the system should revert to
  manual (e.g. switch or mux reconnects real pots) or clamp injection to a safe
  mid-range.
- **Clamp:** Injection voltages must stay within the safe range the transmitter
  expects (e.g. 0–3.3 V or 0–5 V depending on the transmitter); never drive out
  of range.

For implementation details (where to inject, voltage range, mux/switch), open
the transmitter and identify the steering and throttle pot connections and their
voltage range; then drive those nodes from the MCU when in AUTO mode.

## Parrot Bebop 2 Skycontroller

The Parrot Bebop 2 Skycontroller is **not** a flight controller. It is a
Linux-based device with Wi-Fi and joysticks that talks to Bebop drones over IP.
It has **no exposed GPIO or servo outputs** and cannot replace a receiver or
drive servos. It could be used as a vision or Wi-Fi control node, but actuation
still requires an external MCU or servo driver. See
[Salvage and zero-budget hardware](../hardware/salvage-and-zero-budget-hardware.mdx).

## Drone / vehicle control (Phase 2+)

For drone or vehicle control beyond pan/tilt, **MAVLink** is the recommended SDK
boundary. Replace the control stack (e.g. flight controller + MAVLink) rather
than reverse-engineering proprietary RF. See
[conversation-gap-analysis-shopping-bom-rc-future-phases](../../internal/decisions/conversation-gap-analysis-shopping-bom-rc-future-phases.md)
and [Future phases outline](../../internal/decisions/future-phases-outline.md).
