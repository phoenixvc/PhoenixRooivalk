# CUAS Sandbox 2026 - Eligibility Assessment

An honest assessment of Phoenix Rooivalk's current readiness for the Canada
CUAS Sandbox 2026 program, including identified gaps and recommended actions.

:::warning Honest Assessment

This document provides a candid evaluation of our readiness. The CUAS Sandbox
is a **government program** with strict requirements. We should only apply for
opportunities matching our current capability level.

:::

---

## TRL 5 Requirement Analysis

The CUAS Sandbox requires **TRL 5 or higher** at time of application. This
means solutions must have:

> "Completed integration of components and validation testing associated with
> TRL 5 must be complete."

### What TRL 5 Means

| TRL Level | Description | What They Want to See |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| TRL 4 | Component validation in lab | Individual parts work |
| **TRL 5** | **Component integration validated** | **Integrated system demonstrated** |
| TRL 6 | System demo in relevant environment | Field-tested prototype |

**Key Point:** They want to **SEE** that you're at level 5 - not just claim it.

## What This Program Is

The CUAS Sandbox 2026 is:
- An **actual Canadian Government** program
- Run by Department of National Defence (DND)
- Through Innovation for Defence Excellence and Security (IDEaS)
- With **real prize money** ($1.75M CAD)
- And **real evaluation** by military end-users

This is not a pitch competition or early-stage accelerator. This is for
**validated prototypes** ready for military testing.
