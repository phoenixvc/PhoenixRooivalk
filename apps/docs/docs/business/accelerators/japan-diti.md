# Japan Defense Innovation Technology Institute (DITI)

Japan's **Defense Innovation Technology Institute (DITI)** is a new defense
technology accelerator modeled after US DARPA and DIU, established in 2024 to
develop agile defense technologies leveraging academia and private sector
expertise.

---

## Program Overview

| Aspect        | Details                                             |
| ------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| **Organizer** | Ministry of Defense (JMOD)                          |
| **Under**     | Acquisition, Technology and Logistics Agency (ATLA) |
| **Launched**  | October 2024                                        |
| **Staff**     | ~100 personnel                                      |
| **Budget**    | $140M+ (establishment)                              |

---

## Mission

DITI uses agile and innovative means to develop defence technologies that will
address future challenges, leveraging expertise from academic and private
sectors - similar to how DARPA and DIU operate in the United States.

## Market Context

### Japanese Defense Budget

Japan has significantly increased defense spending:

- 2024: $54.1B (record high)
- Projected growth through 2027
- Counter-UAS/air defense priority

### Why Counter-UAS Matters

Japan faces:

- Regional drone proliferation
- North Korean UAV threats
- Chinese UAS activity
- Critical infrastructure protection needs
