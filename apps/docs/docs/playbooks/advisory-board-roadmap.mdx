# Advisory Board Roadmap

**Goal**: Recruit 2-3 credible advisors in 3 weeks who add real value and
investor confidence.

---

## Why Advisors Matter

| Advisor Type         | What Investors See                                            |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Defense/Military** | "They understand the customer and regulatory landscape"       |
| **Aviation/Airport** | "They have industry connections we can leverage"              |
| **Blockchain/Web3**  | "They know how to build token economics and crypto community" |
| **Hardware/D2C**     | "They've scaled consumer products before"                     |

**The formula:**

> 2-3 advisors Ã— relevant experience = reduced execution risk in investor's mind

## The Advisor Profiles You Need

### Priority 1: Defense/Aviation Expert

- Former SANDF (South African National Defence Force)

- Airport security management

## Week 1: Identify & Reach Out (Days 1-7)

### Day 1-2: Build Target List

Create a spreadsheet with 15-20 potential advisors:

| Name          | Background           | Why Them                | LinkedIn | Mutual Connections |
| ------------- | -------------------- | ----------------------- | -------- | ------------------ |
| Col. X (Ret.) | Former SANDF Signals | Defense network         | [link]   | 2 connections      |
| Jane Doe      | Ex-Dedrone           | C-UAS direct experience | [link]   | 1 connection       |
| John Crypto   | Solana ecosystem     | Built DeFi protocol     | [link]   | 3 connections      |

**Research checklist:**

- [ ] Read their LinkedIn thoroughly
- [ ] Google their name + company
- [ ] Check if they advise other startups
- [ ] Find mutual connections

---

### Day 3-5: Warm Introductions

**Best path:** Mutual connection introduces you

**Email to mutual connection:**

```
Subject: Quick favor - intro to [Advisor Name]?

Hi [Name],

I saw you're connected to [Advisor Name] on LinkedIn. We're building drone capture technology at Phoenix Rooivalk, and their background in [specific thing] is exactly what we need.

Would you be comfortable making an intro? Happy to send you a blurb to forward.

Thanks!
[Your name]
```

**Blurb for them to forward:**

```
"[Name] is building drone detection/capture tech for airports. They're raising a seed round and looking for advisors with [defense/blockchain/hardware] experience. Worth a conversation if you're open to it."
```

---

### Day 5-7: Cold Outreach

**LinkedIn message (if no mutual connection):**

```
Hi [Name],

I've been following your work at [Company/Project] - particularly [specific thing they did].

We're building counter-drone technology at Phoenix Rooivalk (edge AI detection + net capture). We're raising our seed round and looking to build an advisory board.

Given your experience in [specific area], I'd love to get 15 minutes of your perspective. No pitch - genuinely looking for advice on [specific challenge].

Would you be open to a quick call?

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Key principles:**

- Be specific about why THEM
- Lead with asking for advice, not asking to be an advisor
- Mention something specific they've done
- Keep it short

---

## Week 2: Conversations & Value Exchange (Days 8-14)

### Day 8-10: Discovery Calls

**Agenda for first call (30 min):**

1. 5 min: Your background, how you found them
2. 10 min: Ask about their experience, current focus
3. 10 min: Share Phoenix Rooivalk, ask for advice
4. 5 min: "Would you be interested in staying involved?"

**Questions to ask:**

- "What's keeping you busy these days?"
- "What do you wish you'd known when you were at our stage?"
- "Who else should I be talking to?"
- "What would you do differently if you were us?"

**What you're evaluating:**

- Do they actually know the space?
- Are they genuinely interested?
- Do they have relevant connections?
- Will they respond to emails?

---

### Day 11-14: The Soft Ask

**After a good conversation:**

```
"I really appreciate your perspective on [specific thing]. This is exactly the kind of insight we need as we grow.

We're building out an advisory board - nothing too formal, but people we can call on for strategic guidance a few times a year.

Would you be open to being an advisor? I can send over what that would look like."
```

**If they say yes:**

- Send advisor agreement (template below)
- Propose equity/compensation
- Schedule follow-up call

**If they hesitate:**

- "No pressure at all. Would it help if I sent you more info on what we're
  building?"
- "What would you need to see to feel comfortable?"
- Move on if not interested

## Week 3: Formalize & Announce (Days 15-21)

### Day 15-17: Advisor Agreement

**Standard terms:**

| Term           | Typical Range   | Our Recommendation |
| -------------- | --------------- | ------------------ |
| **Equity**     | 0.1% - 1.0%     | 0.25% - 0.5%       |
| **Vesting**    | 1-2 years       | 2 years, monthly   |
| **Cliff**      | 3-6 months      | 3 months           |
| **Commitment** | 2-5 hours/month | 2-4 hours/month    |
| **Duration**   | 1-2 years       | 2 years            |

**Advisor Agreement Template:**

```markdown
ADVISOR AGREEMENT

Date: [Date]

Between:

- Phoenix Rooivalk (Pty) Ltd ("Company")
- [Advisor Name] ("Advisor")

1. ROLE Advisor agrees to provide strategic guidance in the area of [specific
   area].

2. COMMITMENT

- Approximately [2-4] hours per month
- Available for reasonable email/phone consultation
- Quarterly check-in calls with founders
- Introductions to relevant contacts when appropriate

3. COMPENSATION

- [0.25-0.5]% equity in the Company
- Subject to standard advisor vesting: 2 years, monthly vesting, 3-month cliff

4. TERM

- 2 years from signing
- Either party may terminate with 30 days notice

5. CONFIDENTIALITY Advisor agrees to keep Company information confidential.

6. NO CONFLICTS Advisor confirms no conflicts with current roles.

---

Company Advisor Date: Date:
```

### Day 18-19: Onboarding

**Send to new advisors:**

1. Company overview deck (pitch deck works)
2. Access to key documents (Google Drive folder)
3. Intro to co-founders (if they haven't met)
4. Calendar invite for first quarterly call

**Set expectations:**

```
"For our first quarter, here's where we'd love your help:
1. [Specific ask - e.g., intro to 2-3 airport security contacts]
2. [Specific ask - e.g., review our blockchain architecture]
3. [Specific ask - e.g., feedback on D2C launch strategy]

We'll check in monthly by email and do a deeper call each quarter."
```

## Compensation Guidelines

| Stage         | Typical Equity | Notes                     |
| ------------- | -------------- | ------------------------- |
| **Pre-seed**  | 0.5% - 1.0%    | Advisors taking more risk |
| **Seed**      | 0.25% - 0.5%   | Standard range            |
| **Series A+** | 0.1% - 0.25%   | Company more de-risked    |

**Alternatives to equity:**

- Cash retainer ($500-2000/month) - rare at seed
- Success fees (% of deals they source)
- Hybrid (smaller equity + cash for specific projects)

## Red Flags to Avoid

| Red Flag                     | Why It's Bad                             |
| ---------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Wants too much equity (>1%)  | Advisors should be cheap                 |
| Wants a board seat           | Advisors advise, they don't govern       |
| No specific expertise        | Name-only advisors add no value          |
| Advises 20+ companies        | Won't have time for you                  |
| Wants salary at seed stage   | Cash-strapped startups can't afford this |
| Won't sign advisor agreement | Not taking it seriously                  |

## How to Get Value from Advisors

### Monthly check-in email:

```
Subject: Phoenix Rooivalk - December Update

Hi [Name],

Quick update:
- [Win]: Signed LOI with Lanseria Airport
- [Challenge]: Struggling to find Jetson Orin supply
- [Ask]: Any connections to NVIDIA distributors in SA?

No call needed unless you have 10 minutes.

Thanks,
[Your name]
```

### Quarterly call agenda:

1. 10 min: Company update (wins, challenges, metrics)
2. 15 min: Deep dive on one topic where you need their help
3. 5 min: Specific asks (intros, reviews, etc.)
