# Pre-Pitch Checklist

**Use this checklist before every investor pitch to ensure you're fully prepared.**

---

## 1 Week Before

### Materials
- [ ] Pitch deck exported to PDF (backup)
- [ ] Leave-behind one-pager printed (5 copies)
- [ ] Business cards ready
- [ ] Demo videos downloaded to device (not streaming)
- [ ] Backup of all materials on USB drive

### Research
- [ ] Research the investors attending
- [ ] Note their portfolio companies (any synergies?)
- [ ] Prepare 1-2 personalized questions for them
- [ ] Check recent news about the firm

### Practice
- [ ] Full run-through with timer (3 min and 5 min versions)
- [ ] Practice Q&A with a colleague
- [ ] Record yourself and review
- [ ] Get feedback on pacing and clarity

---

## 1 Day Before

### Logistics
- [ ] Confirm meeting time and location
- [ ] Know how to get there (parking, building access)
- [ ] Test video call link if virtual
- [ ] Charge all devices

### Final Prep
- [ ] Review the investor's recent investments
- [ ] Re-read your Q&A prep section
- [ ] Prepare your "ask" clearly in your head
- [ ] Get a good night's sleep

### Materials Check
- [ ] Laptop charged (100%)
- [ ] Phone charged (backup for hotspot)
- [ ] Printed leave-behinds in folder
- [ ] Pen and notepad for notes

---

## Day Of (2 Hours Before)

### Personal
- [ ] Eat a light meal (avoid sugar crash)
- [ ] Hydrate well
- [ ] Dress appropriately (smart casual for most VC)
- [ ] Arrive 15 minutes early

### Tech Setup
- [ ] Test laptop display/HDMI connection
- [ ] Test audio if using video demos
- [ ] Close unnecessary apps (notifications off)
- [ ] Open pitch deck, ready to present
- [ ] Have demo videos in separate window, ready

### Mental Prep
- [ ] Review your opening 30 seconds
- [ ] Review your closing "ask"
- [ ] Take 5 deep breaths
- [ ] Remind yourself: you know this better than anyone

---

## Just Before (5 Minutes)

### Quick Checks
- [ ] Phone on silent (not vibrate)
- [ ] Laptop notifications off
- [ ] Water nearby
- [ ] Smile and relax shoulders

### Opening Ready
- [ ] Know your first sentence by heart
- [ ] Make eye contact when entering
- [ ] Firm handshake, introduce yourself clearly

---

## During the Pitch

### Delivery Reminders
- [ ] Start with the problem (hook them immediately)
- [ ] Speak slower than you think you need to
- [ ] Pause after key points
- [ ] Watch their body language
- [ ] If they interrupt with a question, answer it (don't defer)

### Time Management
- [ ] 3-min pitch: 5 slides, ~35s each
- [ ] 5-min pitch: 6 slides, ~45s each
- [ ] Leave 30% of time for Q&A
- [ ] If running over, skip to the ask

### Common Traps
- [ ] Don't read from slides
- [ ] Don't apologize for anything
- [ ] Don't say "I think" - say "We will"
- [ ] Don't oversell - be confident but honest

---

## After the Pitch

### Immediate (Same Day)
- [ ] Send thank-you email within 4 hours
- [ ] Include any materials they requested
- [ ] Note their questions/concerns for follow-up
- [ ] Debrief: What went well? What to improve?

### Follow-Up (Within 1 Week)
- [ ] Send any additional materials promised
- [ ] Connect on LinkedIn if appropriate
- [ ] Schedule follow-up call if interest expressed
- [ ] Update your CRM/tracker

## If Things Go Wrong

| Situation | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| **Tech fails** | "Let me walk you through this verbally while we fix it." Pull out printed one-pager. |
| **Running out of time** | "I want to respect your time. Let me skip to the ask and we can cover details in follow-up." |
| **Tough question you don't know** | "That's a great question. I don't have that number off the top of my head, but I'll follow up today." |
| **They seem disengaged** | Ask a question: "Does this resonate with what you're seeing in the market?" |
| **Multiple interruptions** | They're interested. Answer each question fully, then ask "Should I continue or dive deeper here?" |
